📘 EMI Formula & Important Information

📐 How EMI is Calculated

EMI is calculated using a standard loan formula used by banks worldwide.

EMI = [P × r × (1 + r)^n] / [(1 + r)^n − 1]

Where:
• P = Loan amount (Principal)
• r = Monthly interest rate
• n = Total number of months

⚠️ Disclaimer

This EMI calculator is for educational purposes only. Actual EMI may vary based on bank policies, credit score, interest rate changes, and processing fees.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common EMI and loan-related questions.

What is EMI?

EMI is a fixed monthly payment used to repay loans including principal and interest.

How is EMI calculated?

EMI is calculated using loan amount, interest rate, and tenure using a standard financial formula.

Can I reduce EMI?

Yes, by increasing tenure, reducing interest rate, or making prepayments.

Is prepayment useful?

Yes, it reduces total interest and helps close loans faster.

Does EMI include interest?

Yes, EMI includes both principal and interest components.

Fixed vs floating EMI?

Fixed EMI stays constant, floating EMI changes based on market rates.
